Forecasting Temperature and Precipitation in New York

Using historical weather data collected by U.S. weather stations (Source: NOAA), our group created machine learning models to forecast temperature and precipitation in New York.

During the project, I obtained NYC weather data with NOAA APIs and from CSV reports, cleaned the datasets, engineered features (e.g. lag data and moving averages), performed hyperparameter tuning and trained XGBoost models with different feature sets, and evaluated model performance to select the best models for "production".

Detecting Disaster Tweets using Natural Language Processing

Research question: Is it possible to detect which written tweets are about a disaster so people are informed early and can communicate with one another to save lives?

In this age where posting on social media and pulling out the phone to record bizarre things one encounters has become common, social media has become an unexpected source for quickly getting the latest updates about a disaster. However, promotional tweets can overpopulate searches about a disaster and delay detection of real tweets about disasters.

In this project, my team and I sought to build supervised classifier models that can differentiate between disaster and non-disaster-related tweets. I cleaned Twitter data from Kaggle and built Naive Bayes and XGBoost models, comparing their performance with my teammate's. A successful model could become an important tool for fast community and authoritative action towards a disaster.

Predicting 311 Call Resolution Time

For our Business Analytics Capstone project, my team and I decided to look into the problem of abnormally long 311 call resolution time. Specifically, we attempted to build supervised regression models that can predict 311 call resolution times.

In theory, the predictions would reflect normal resolution times. If a call, in actuality, exceeds the prediction by a lot, we can conclude that the resolution of the issue in the call should be prioritized since it is outside of normal processing time.

Additionally, we wanted to see what factors play a strong role in determining the resolution time for a call, thereby identifying any biases that may be at play.

Role: Data cleaning, exploratory analysis, data visualization, building tree-based models, evaluating performance, and reporting
